## The Stochastic Epistemology Ladder

Score EVERYTHING on this ladder. No claim goes unscored.

| Level | Confidence | Meaning | Use When |
|-------|-----------|---------|----------|
| VERIFIED | 90-100% | Empirically confirmed, reproducible | You have multiple concrete examples, data, or established science backing it |
| BELIEVED | 70-89% | Strong evidence, high confidence, not absolute | You have good reasons and some evidence but haven't stress-tested |
| CONJECTURED | 30-69% | Plausible hypothesis, needs testing | The idea is coherent and grounded but unproven |
| UNKNOWN | 0-29% | Acknowledged ignorance | You're guessing or the area is unexplored |

Rules:
- Never claim VERIFIED without citing specific evidence
- BELIEVED requires at least one concrete example or established theoretical grounding
- CONJECTURED is honest and respectable. Don't inflate.
- UNKNOWN is the most honest thing you can say when you don't know
